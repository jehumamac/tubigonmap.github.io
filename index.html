Tubigon, Bohol

Tubigon is a municipality located in the province of Bohol, Philippines. It is situated on the western part of the island of Bohol and has a population of over 50,000 people.

Tubigon is a coastal town that serves as a gateway to other popular tourist destinations in Bohol such as the Chocolate Hills, the Tarsier Sanctuary, and the beaches of Panglao Island. It is also known for its agricultural produce, particularly rice, corn, and coconut.

One of the most notable landmarks in Tubigon is the St. Isidore the Farmer Parish Church, which features a bell tower that was built during the Spanish colonial period in the Philippines. The town also has a public market where visitors can buy local produce and handicrafts.

Tubigon has a bustling port area that serves as a major transportation hub for inter-island ferries, with regular trips to and from Cebu City and other parts of the Visayas region. This makes Tubigon a convenient stopover for travelers who want to explore the rest of Bohol and the surrounding islands.

Overall, Tubigon offers a mix of natural attractions, cultural landmarks, and modern amenities, making it a worthwhile destination for visitors to Bohol.
